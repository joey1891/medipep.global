Technical FAQs

Porcine vs. Human Dermal Matrix

Question 01

Why does Medifab use Porcine Dermis instead of Human Dermis?

While Human Allograft Dermal Matrix (HADM) is often considered a standard, Porcine Xenograft Dermal Matrix (PADM) offers significant structural, mechanical, and logistical advantages that make it a superior regenerative platform.

1. Structural & Biomechanical Superiority

Pigs are the closest physiological match to humans in terms of skin layers, thickness, and collagen composition. Crucially, porcine dermis exhibits a highly anisotropic collagen arrangement (Orientation Index $\approx 0.61$) compared to the isotropic human dermis ($OI \approx 0.08$). This directional alignment provides much higher tensile strength and resistance to mechanical load, which is critical for tissue reconstruction.

Question 02

How does Medifab solve the immunogenicity issue of Xenografts?

The biggest challenge for xenografts is the risk of immune rejection. Medifab's core innovation lies in our Supercritical Carbon Dioxide ($sCO_2$) processing.

Fundamental Decellularization

Unlike traditional chemical methods that can damage the matrix, $sCO_2$ penetrates deep into the tissue to effectively remove cellular components, lipids, and DNA while preserving delicate structural proteins and growth factors.

Safety & Purity

This eco-friendly process leaves zero chemical residues and achieves maximum biological safety, making it as safe as—or even safer than—human-derived tissue that carries risks of HIV or Hepatitis transmission from donors.

Question 03

What are the economic and regulatory advantages?

  • Predictable Quality: Human tissue quality varies wildly based on donor age and lifestyle. Porcine sources are standardized through strictly controlled breeding and age management, ensuring consistent clinical outcomes.
  • Scalability: Porcine dermis is an industrial-scale resource, allowing for "Off-the-shelf" supply and significantly lower costs compared to expensive, donation-dependent human grafts.
  • Regulatory Compliance: We adhere to strict international standards (ISO 22442) and MFDS guidelines for high-risk (Class IV) medical devices, ensuring every batch is virus-free and safe for human implantation.
